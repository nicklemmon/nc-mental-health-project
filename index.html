What is mental health treatment?

Getting care starts with learning what to ask for

In the current system, level of care is based on a client's need. The following is a list of the most common services in North Carolina. This is not an exhaustive list, but ideally, each of these services are available in every county across the state.

Outpatient therapy
Traditional talk therapy in an office-based setting. Therapy is an effective tool for helping clients build the skills needed to manage independently on a day-to-day basis. There is state-funding to pay for this service for uninsured residents of North Carolina, but the number of sessions is limited.
Outpatient medication management
Typically the role of psychiatry, medication management often complements other services through the prescription of medication. Medication offers symptom relief which can help promote stability, and ideally, recovery. There is also state funding for this service for uninsured residents, though sessions tend to be brief and with loq frequency.
Psychosocial rehabilitation
PSR for short, these programs offer a form of social structure through a local clubhouse model. Typically these programs are reserved for individuals with serious mental illness, PSR offers a community-based alternative for individuals who may have previously been institutionalized.
Assertive Community Treatment Team (ACTT)
ACTT is the highest level of community-based care and typically reserved for clients with serious mental illness and resulting functional impairments. These teams are made up of social workers, psychiatrists, nurses, and other qualified professionals who help clients maintain stability by providing wraparound services.
